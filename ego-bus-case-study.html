Project Overview

This case study outlines the design of the eGo Bus mobile application, a hackathon project aimed at improving the public transportation experience. The goal was to create a user-friendly app for booking bus tickets, tracking buses in real-time, and accessing relevant travel information.

My Role

UX Designer

Team

Hackathon Project

Duration

2024 (4 months)

Tools Used

Figma, User Research, Prototyping

The Problem

Public transportation users often face challenges such as difficulty in booking tickets, lack of real-time bus tracking, and insufficient information about routes and schedules. The eGo Bus app aimed to address these pain points by providing a one-stop solution for a seamless and stress-free travel experience.

The Process

The design process for the eGo Bus app involved user research to understand the needs and frustrations of bus travelers. This was followed by creating user personas, journey maps, and wireframes to define the app's features and user flow. High-fidelity prototypes were then developed in Figma and tested with users to gather feedback and make iterative improvements.

The Solution

The eGo Bus app provides a simple and intuitive interface for booking bus tickets, with features like seat selection and online payments. The app also includes real-time bus tracking, allowing users to see the exact location of their bus and plan their travel accordingly. Additionally, the app provides detailed information about bus routes, schedules, and fares, making it a comprehensive tool for bus travelers.

Outcomes & Impact

The eGo Bus app was a successful hackathon project that demonstrated the potential of a user-centered design approach to improve the public transportation experience. The app received positive feedback from users and judges, and it highlighted the importance of features like real-time tracking and easy booking in a modern transportation app.
